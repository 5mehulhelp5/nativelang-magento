<h1>Top 10 Hilly Views in Chiang Mai: Photos and Routes</h1>

<h2>Introduction</h2>
<p>Chiang Mai, a beautiful city nestled in the mountains of northern Thailand, offers breathtaking hilly views that attract tourists from all over the world. Whether you are an adventure seeker or a nature lover, Chiang Mai's hilly landscapes are sure to captivate your heart. In this article, we will explore the top 10 hilly views in Chiang Mai, complete with stunning photos and suggested routes to make your visit unforgettable.</p>

<h2>1. Doi Suthep</h2>
<p>One of the most iconic hilly views in Chiang Mai is Doi Suthep, a majestic mountain located just outside the city. To reach the summit, you can either hike up the stairs or take a cable car. Once you reach the top, you will be rewarded with a panoramic view of the city and the surrounding lush greenery.</p>

<h2>2. Doi Inthanon</h2>
<p>Doi Inthanon, also known as "The Roof of Thailand," offers stunning views from its peak, which is the highest point in the country. Visitors can explore the beautiful trails and witness the awe-inspiring sunrise or sunset over the mountains. Don't forget to bring your camera to capture the breathtaking scenery.</p>

<h2>3. Wat Phra That Doi Kham</h2>
<p>Perched atop a hill, Wat Phra That Doi Kham provides a stunning view of Chiang Mai's cityscape. This temple is also known as the "Temple of the Golden Mountain," and it offers a serene atmosphere where visitors can immerse themselves in the beauty of the surroundings.</p>

<h2>4. Wat Phra That Doi Suthep</h2>
<p>Located near Doi Suthep mountain, Wat Phra That Doi Suthep is a renowned Buddhist temple that offers a majestic view of Chiang Mai and its surrounding areas. The temple is adorned with intricate details and golden decorations, making it a must-visit attraction for tourists.</p>

<h2>5. Mon Cham</h2>
<p>Mon Cham, also known as "Little Switzerland," is a hilltop village that offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. Visitors can enjoy the cool breeze while strolling through the flower gardens and savoring the local cuisine.</p>

<h2>6. Wat Palad</h2>
<p>Hidden in the forest, Wat Palad provides a peaceful retreat for those seeking a tranquil hilly view. The temple is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a picturesque view of the mountains. Exploring the temple grounds and its serene atmosphere is an experience not to be missed.</p>

<h2>7. Huay Kaew Waterfall</h2>
<p>Located near Doi Suthep, Huay Kaew Waterfall offers a refreshing escape from the city's hustle and bustle. The waterfall cascades down the hill, creating a serene and picturesque view. Visitors can relax by the waterfall and enjoy the tranquility of nature.</p>

<h2>8. Wat Umong</h2>
<p>Wat Umong, also known as the "Tunnel Temple," is a unique temple located in the forested foothills of Doi Suthep. The temple is known for its ancient tunnels and serene surroundings, offering visitors a peaceful hilly view away from the crowds.</p>

<h2>9. Wat Phra That Si Chom Thong</h2>
<p>Located in the district of Chom Thong, Wat Phra That Si Chom Thong offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding countryside. The temple is perched on a hill and provides a peaceful atmosphere where visitors can admire the natural beauty of Chiang Mai.</p>

<h2>10. Wat Phra That Sri Jom Tong</h2>
<p>Situated in the Mae Rim district, Wat Phra That Sri Jom Tong offers a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The temple is adorned with intricate golden decorations and provides a serene hilly view that will leave you in awe.</p>

<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>Chiang Mai's hilly views are nothing short of breathtaking. From the iconic Doi Suthep to the hidden gems like Wat Palad and Mon Cham, each location offers a unique perspective of the city and its natural beauty. Don't forget to bring your camera to capture these unforgettable moments. Plan your visit to Chiang Mai and explore these top 10 hilly views to create memories that will last a lifetime.</p>
